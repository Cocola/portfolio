## Context

PennyPet is a B2C app for *pet parents*: a bank account with partner cashback and
flat-rate pet insurance. Several redesigns handed to outside vendors had led nowhere; only
one visual lead held up, a pink-violet gradient for highlighted text, and it was
underused.

I proposed going all the way: a complete visual world, a modernised brand image, and
reworked flows. The launch of a gamification feature was the ideal way in for the redesign.

## Typography

Work Sans, the original typeface, lacked character. I replaced it with Outfit, more
contemporary and distinctive, and reworked the typographic hierarchy (sizes, weights,
spacing) for legibility and balance across the screens.

## Colour

The pink-violet gradient becomes the centrepiece: it paces the interface and highlights
key actions and the gamification. I rationalised the secondary tints into a coherent,
accessible, easily-extensible palette, which also carries the presentation of the three
plans. Accessibility was held to at least WCAG AA.

## Design System

I built a full design system in Figma, backed by Tailwind tokens through a community file:
a direct match between design and code, fewer gaps, faster development. Six main screen
templates were enough to cover nearly 190 screens, maximising reuse.

## Development

The UI redesign came with a full React Native refactor:

- migrating NativeWind v3 to v4;
- rebuilding the ~190 screens on the new templates;
- a unified component library.

Shipping screens became noticeably faster and more reliable. About five weeks were enough
to migrate the whole app.

## Impact

The new identity was very well received by users and investors alike, strengthening
PennyPet’s credibility. Internally, the design system and templates brought a clear
productivity gain (roughly **200%** across design and dev) while clarifying the technical
stack: **190 screens** rebuilt, **6 reusable templates**, in **5 weeks**.
