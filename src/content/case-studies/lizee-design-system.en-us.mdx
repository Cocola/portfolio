## Context

In 2021, Lizee had no in-house resource to design the interfaces of its warehouse
circular-logistics software. The first screens were designed and built on the framework
already in place, Material UI. Faced with growing feature demand and a real need for better
ergonomics, Lizee decided to overhaul the whole app in a V2, whose first HiFi mockups were
delivered by an outside vendor.

As screens multiplied, building a design system became urgent to guarantee maintenance and
consistency across mockup production.

## Typography

Hanken Grotesk, taken from the logo, is the primary typeface: its weights cover both buttons
and body copy. Oswald sets numeric data apart (dates, statistics). To avoid a web-performance
hit, only its digits are loaded, which makes the file much lighter.

## Colour

The night-blue brand colour is primary, a light green secondary. Semantic colours handle the
various states, all ranged from light to dark (50 to 900). Contrast meets at least WCAG 2.0 AA.

## Components

Some components (modals, sheets, boxes) rely on *instance swap*: instances are never
detached, which keeps a consistent style through every design-system update.

## Impact

Since 2022 the design system has evolved continuously; it even absorbed a rebrand without a
hitch. Every mockup rests on a single Figma library, and new features on the same component
base, shipped much faster. In numbers: **3 weeks** on average for a feature’s HiFi mockups,
**18 features** built on the design system, **2 products** (a B2B SaaS and an internal app)
relying on it, and a **~200% productivity gain** (production time cut in half).
